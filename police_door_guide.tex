\begin{center}
\Huge\bfseries WHEN POLICE ARE AT THE DOOR
\end{center}

\begin{center}
\large\textbf{YOU ARE NOT REQUIRED TO OPEN THE DOOR}\\
Unless police have a valid warrant, you can legally ignore them.
\end{center}

\section{IF YOU CHOOSE NOT TO ANSWER (SAFEST OPTION)}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item[\textcolor{darkgreen}{\checkmark}] \textbf{Stay silent inside your home}
    \item[\textcolor{darkgreen}{\checkmark}] \textbf{Check your security camera to confirm it's police}
    \item[\textcolor{darkgreen}{\checkmark}] \textbf{Do not make noise or indicate you're home}
    \item[\textcolor{darkgreen}{\checkmark}] \textbf{Police will eventually leave if they don't have a warrant}
\end{itemize}

\section{IF POLICE CLAIM THEY HAVE A WARRANT}

\subsection{STEP 1: Ask to See the Warrant}

Say: \textbf{``Do you have a warrant? I want to see it.''}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Ask them to slide it under the door or hold it up to a window/camera
    \item Check if it's signed by a judge
    \item Check if it lists your specific address
    \item Check what they're authorized to search for
    \item \textbf{If they can't show you a warrant, DO NOT open the door}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{STEP 2: State You Do NOT Consent}

Say clearly: \textbf{``I do not consent to any search of my home, my person, or my property.''}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Even with a warrant, state this explicitly
    \item Repeat it if they ask to search areas not listed in the warrant
    \item This protects your ability to challenge the search later
\end{itemize}

\subsection{STEP 3: Ask Clarifying Questions (Optional)}

If safe to do so, ask:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item ``What exactly is this warrant for?''
    \item ``What specific items or areas are you authorized to search?''
\end{itemize}

This documents if they exceed the warrant's scope.

\subsection{STEP 4: Exercise Your Rights}

Say: \textbf{``I exercise my right to remain silent. I want my lawyer.''}

Then \textbf{STOP TALKING}. Do not answer ANY questions, including:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item ``Do you know why we're here?''
    \item ``Whose [item] is this?''
    \item ``When did you last see [person]?''
\end{itemize}

\subsection{STEP 5: Document Everything}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Record everything on your phone if possible
    \item Note which officers are present and where they search
    \item Photograph any damage immediately after they leave
    \item Request a written inventory of anything they take
    \item Save all recordings and the warrant copy
\end{itemize}

\section{IF THEY DON'T HAVE A WARRANT (KNOCK-AND-TALK)}

\subsection{BEFORE Opening:}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{Ask}: ``Do you have a warrant?''
    \begin{itemize}
        \item If NO: You do not have to open the door or speak to them
    \end{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Use your security camera} to see and speak without opening the door
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{IF You Open the Door:}

\textbf{DO:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Keep screen door/security door LOCKED between you and police
    \item Stay INSIDE your home at all times
    \item Start recording on your phone immediately
    \item Say clearly: \textbf{``I do not consent to any searches''}
    \item Say: \textbf{``I exercise my right to remain silent''}
    \item Say: \textbf{``I will not answer questions without my attorney''}
\end{itemize}

\textbf{DON'T:}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Step outside (you lose legal protections)
    \item Let them inside without a warrant
    \item Answer any questions
    \item Make small talk or explain yourself
    \item Touch or physically resist the officer
    \item Get angry or sarcastic
\end{itemize}

\subsection{IF They Won't Leave:}

Say firmly: \textbf{``I'm asking you to leave my property now. If you don't have a warrant, you're trespassing.''}

Then close and lock your door. Document everything on video.

\subsection{IF They Put Their Foot in the Door:}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item \textbf{DO NOT} try to force the door closed on their foot
    \item Continue recording everything
    \item State clearly: \textbf{``You do not have my consent to enter. You are trespassing.''}
    \item Exercise your right to remain silent
    \item Call your attorney if possible
\end{itemize}

\section{WHAT NOT TO DO:}

\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item[\textcolor{darkred}{\texttimes}] Never invite police inside
    \item[\textcolor{darkred}{\texttimes}] Never step outside to talk
    \item[\textcolor{darkred}{\texttimes}] Never try to explain or justify yourself
    \item[\textcolor{darkred}{\texttimes}] Never sign anything without reading it and consulting a lawyer
    \item[\textcolor{darkred}{\texttimes}] Never physically resist (document verbally instead)
\end{itemize}

\section{EXIGENT CIRCUMSTANCES (EMERGENCIES)}

Police CAN enter without a warrant ONLY if:
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Someone is in immediate danger
    \item Evidence is actively being destroyed
    \item A suspect is fleeing into your home
    \item There's a violent disturbance (loud party, domestic violence call)
\end{itemize}

\section{AFTER A POLICE SEARCH:}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=*]
    \item Write down everything while it's fresh (time, what was said, what was searched)
    \item Get officer names and badge numbers
    \item Photograph all damage
    \item Save the inventory list and warrant copy
    \item Back up all video footage
    \item \textbf{Consult a lawyer immediately} --- you may be able to challenge the search or file a civil rights lawsuit
\end{enumerate}
